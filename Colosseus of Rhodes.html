What is the Colossus of Rhodes?


The Colossus of Rhodes is a statue of Helios, the Sun God of Greece. Found on Rhodes, a small island of greece, it is one of the seven wonders, because at the time it was the biggest statue in the world. It was built to celebrate the successful defense of Rhodes against enemy invaders.

The man who constructed it is Charles of Lindos, a famous greek Sculptor. It was made in 280 BC, or about 2300 years ago.
